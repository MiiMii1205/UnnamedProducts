# Unnamed Products #

Adds unnamed branded products to PEAK from a certain famous Canadian store brand.

## 📕 "Unnamed" Origins ##

"unnamed" is in essence a parody/hommage to
a [certain famous Canadian store brand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/No_Name_(brand)) created in the 1970s.

The brand got its name from its "no frills" vibe and it iconic and simple design, providing customers with a lower cost
alternative from your typical brand.

Despite the brand iconic status, the brand is often regarded as "cheap" and have lower quality assurance due to their
low costs.

## 🎮 How it works ##

Whenever you find an item in luggage, it will have a chance of being "unnamed".

You can identify an unnamed item by its simple, bright yellow packaging with black lettering.

Whenever you use an unnamed item, there's a random chance of it being either weaker or stronger than its "official" version.

For example, let's take the airline food.

An unnamed airline food can restore between **80%** and **120%** of hunger and give between **80%** and **120%** of extra stamina
compared to official airline food.

However, for items with multiple uses, the same item can be weak one use and strong the next.

For example, using unnamed scout cookies might give you **80%** of extra stamina the first time, but the next use might be **120%**
of extra stamina instead!

But that's not all! There are tons of unnamed items, and some have unique mechanics, each with its own pros and cons.
We'll let you discover them out yourself.

## 💼 Unnamed Luggage ##

Whenever you start a run, some luggage will spawn as unnamed luggage. 

You can identify unnamed luggage with their iconic yellow and black color scheme.

<figure>
  <img alt="Unnamed Luggage" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/MiiMii1205/UnnamedProducts/refs/heads/master/imgs/unnamedluggage.png" height="512" />
  <figcaption>An small unnamed luggage</figcaption>
</figure>

Which luggage spawns as unnamed luggage is determined by the map rotation. So the same luggage will always spawn unnamed within the same map rotation.

The items you find inside unnamed luggage will **_always_** be unnamed branded.

Otherwise, they function just like their vanilla counterpart. Large luggage have more/rarer items than small ones and so on. 

You can also encounter unnamed Ancient Statues (identifiable with its bright yellow coloring), which will **_always_** give you unnamed mystical items.

## ✈️ Unnamed Airplane ##

When starting a run, there's also a chance the crashed airplane at the shore will be unnamed.

Unnamed airplanes can be easily identified with their yellow and black color scheme.

<figure>
  <img alt="Chrashed unnamed airplane" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/MiiMii1205/UnnamedProducts/refs/heads/master/imgs/crashedairplane.png" height="512" />
  <figcaption>An unnamed airplane at the Shore</figcaption>
</figure>


Whether the shore airplane is unnamed or not depends on the map rotation, just like unnamed luggage.

When an airplane is unnamed, all items inside its wreckage will be unnamed.

What does this mean in practice? You’ll have to find out yourself.

## 🔌 Mod Compatibility ##

Aside of supporting vanilla items, the mod also supports the following list of mods:

- [Scouting4Food](https://thunderstore.io/c/peak/p/Scouting4Mods/Scouting4Food/)
- [CanadianCuisine](https://thunderstore.io/c/peak/p/OracleTeam/CanadianCuisine/)

Whenever you play using any of these mods, you might find some unique "unnamed" variants of those mod's items.

As always, they are identifiable with their unnamed branding, and have the same randomized stats.

### ⚠️ Item Stat Displays ###

When playing with the mod installed, mods like [ItemStats](https://thunderstore.io/c/peak/p/boxofbiscuits97/ItemStats/) won't show the actual stats.

This is because unnamed buffs/debuffs are **_per use_**. 

So if you have those kind of mods, don't trust what's being displayed! Because just like store-branded products, sometimes it's not really precise.
